02 / 07 · Ad creative
01 / Ad creative

Three fixes that move CPL in AU AC + heatpumps.

Most HVAC ads fail at the same place: they show a unit and a sticker price. Homeowners are buying a comfortable home and a smaller power bill, not specs. They don't know SEER ratings or refrigerant types. Match the ad to what they actually want.

Fix 01 · Angle

Lead with comfort and bill savings, not the unit.

"Cut your heating bill by 40% with reverse cycle" beats "Daikin 7.1kW for $2,490" every time. Lead with the savings story, or the comfort story (silent operation, even temperatures, fast cooldown). The model name and price come after the click, not before.

Fix 02 · Pricing frame

Quote in installments, not lump sums.

$3,500 upfront kills the click. $34/week absorbs into the budget like the bill they already pay. Reframe pricing as a weekly or monthly installment, and tie it back to the bill it offsets. "From $34/week, less than your current heating bill" outperforms a sticker price every time we test it.

Fix 03 · Imagery

Use real install photos. Skip the AI renders.

AI-generated HVAC imagery looks generic. Wonky split heads on the wrong wall, suspiciously clean ductwork, no Australian outdoor units anywhere. Use phone-shot photos of your actual installs. The mounting bracket, the cable cover, the condenser unit on the side of a brick house. Authenticity beats polish in this niche.

Rule of thumb: your ad headline should describe the future. "Lower bill, warmer house, installed in 2 weeks." The price comes after the click.

03 / 07 · Targeting
02 / Targeting

Target like a local installer, not a generic media buyer.

Most generalist agencies stop at "homeowners 30-65, interested in heating and cooling." That's lazy. Stack postcode, state rebate eligibility, and seasonal timing deliberately. State governments do half the urgency work for you.

Tactic 01 · State rebates

Postcode lists tied to VEU, ESS, and federal STCs.

Victoria runs VEU (Victorian Energy Upgrades): big rebates for replacing gas heaters with reverse cycle, and electric storage hot water with heatpump hot water. NSW runs ESS (Energy Savings Scheme) with similar incentives. Federal SRES STCs apply to heatpump hot water across Australia. Build postcode lists per state and write rebate-specific copy. "Victorian homeowners: $1,000+ off a reverse cycle install via VEU" outperforms a generic "save on heating" line by a wide margin.

Tactic 02 · Audience stack

Income, age, and exclusions.

Layer top 25 to 50% household income, age 35 to 65, and exclude renters via interest stack. For heatpump hot water specifically, lean into older housing stock (1990s and earlier homes are more likely to still run electric storage). For reverse cycle replacing gas, target gas-network suburbs in VIC and NSW. Home renovation, energy efficiency, ducted heating, and wood fireplace interests are reasonable proxies.

Tactic 03 · Seasonal timing

Ride the seasons. Run replacement urgency in the gaps.

Pre-summer (Sept to Nov): hard push on AC replacement, when memory of last summer is fresh. Pre-winter (April to June): heating heatpumps and heatpump hot water. In the shoulder seasons, lean on replacement urgency: "15-year-old units fail at the worst time. Replace before the next heatwave." HVAC ad spend that runs only during peak season leaves money on the table.

Note on rebates: VEU and ESS are state-run schemes that change rates year to year. Federal STCs for heatpump hot water also drop annually as the SRES winds down toward 2030. Verify current rebate values and accreditation requirements with the relevant scheme administrator before publishing specific dollar figures in ads.

04 / 07 · Qualification
03 / Qualification

Five form questions that kill bad leads before your closer sees them.

Cheap leads are expensive when they don't close. The form is your first filter. Five questions, no more. Each one earns its place by removing a specific bad-fit prospect.

1. Are you the homeowner?

Yes/No. Renters never need to enter your CRM. Auto-route a "no" to a polite exit message.

2. What's your postcode?

Maps to VEU eligibility (VIC), ESS eligibility (NSW), and confirms they're inside your install radius before you waste tech drive time.

3. What's your current setup?

Gas ducted / Reverse cycle split / Window unit / None / Electric storage hot water. Routes leads to the right product line (reverse cycle vs heatpump hot water vs new install) and exposes the rebate angle that fits.

4. How old is your current system?

Less than 5 years / 5 to 10 / 10+ / Broken. Anyone under 5 years rarely replaces. 10+ or broken gets prioritised on the calendar.

5. When are you looking to install?

Now / 1 to 3 months / 3 to 6 months / Browsing. The "browsing" answer tags them for nurture, not a call. Closers focus on the first three.

Five short questions filter renters, no-system households, too-new-to-replace, out-of-area leads, and not-ready browsers. That's a lot of waste removed in a 30-second form.

05 / 07 · GHL CRM
04 / GHL CRM

A GoHighLevel pipeline you can build in an afternoon.

Five stages, three automations, one calendar. That's the whole CRM for a solo or small-team HVAC installer. Skip the 50-stage rabbit hole.

The pipeline

5 stages, in order.

01New enquiry. Fresh form submit, unqualified.
02Qualified. Form passed, contact verified by SMS or call.
03Sit booked. In-home estimate on the calendar.
04Quoted. Written quote sent, awaiting decision.
05Installed. Deal closed, system commissioned.
The 3 automations that earn their keep

Speed-to-lead, no-show recovery, long-cycle nurture.

Speed-to-lead SMS, fired within 60 seconds: "Hey [first_name], thanks for the enquiry on [reverse_cycle / heatpump_hot_water] for your home in [suburb]. I'm [closer_name] from [company]. Got 2 minutes to chat now or want me to call tomorrow morning?"

No-show recovery, fired 5 minutes after a missed estimate: "Looks like we missed you, no stress. Want to grab another time? [reschedule_link]"

Long-cycle nurture for "browsing" leads: a 5-touch sequence over 60 days. Soft education on rebate deadlines, replacement urgency for ageing systems, and seasonal timing. No hard pitch. Keeps you front of mind for when the system breaks down or the bill jumps.

Before you launch: pixel and Meta Conversions API verified by the GHL form firing a real test event. Most installers skip this and lose 30% or more of their attribution. Don't be one of them.

Seven days to launch. No fluff.

Most agencies take 60 days. You don't have 60 days. Here's the schedule that gets a properly built HVAC campaign live in a working week.

Day 1

Audit current setup

What's running, what's broken, what to keep. Pull last 90 days of Meta data and current GHL state.

Day 2

Build GHL pipeline and automations

5 stages, 3 automations, calendar wired. Test each automation with a fake submit end-to-end.

Day 3

Set up the lead form and verify pixel

5 qualifying questions live. Pixel and Conversions API confirmed firing on submit, not just on page load.

Day 4

Produce 3 creatives, 3 angles

Bill savings, installment pricing, rebate or seasonal urgency. Static is fine. Real install photos beat polished video in this niche.

Day 5

Build the campaign

2 ad sets under one CBO budget. Audience: top 50% household income, age 35-65, postcode list (state-aware for rebate copy), exclude renters. Each ad set runs a different offer angle head-to-head.

Day 6

Final QA

Form mapped to the right pipeline stage. Calendar showing closer or tech availability. Speed-to-lead SMS firing. Pixel firing. Ad set toggled on.

Day 7

Launch

Go live in the morning. First lead within 24 hours if pixel learning is decent. Don't touch anything for 48 hours after launch. Let it learn.
